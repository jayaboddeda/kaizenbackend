Treatments

External beam radiation

Using a linear accelerator, which is a special X-ray machine, high and precise energy rays are used on the tumors. This machine, without touching the patient, moves around and aims radiation on the cancer. It also ensures minimal damage of the adjacent tissues and organs, thus reducing side effects.


Internal radiation or brachytherapy

Internal radiation or Brachytherapy is a type of treatment that uses radiation to kill cancer cells thereby shrinking tumors. Ribbons, seeds, or wires are the forms of radiation that are put near the cancer or into your body.


External beam radiation therapy

Stereotactic radiosurgery (SRS) & Body radiation therapy (SBRT)

This is a non-invasive radiation therapy where high focused radiation beams are used on tumors to eliminate them in the lungs, neck, spine, brain or any other part of the body.


Intensity-modulated (IMRT)

This is a radiation therapy, an advanced mode of high-precision radiotherapy that is used to stop cancer cells from growing or dividing by damaging their DNA. This leads to slowing down or completely stopping tumor growth.

In most cases, all the cancer cells are killed leaving no chance for regrowth of cancer cells.


Image-guided (IGRT)

This is a radiation therapy where imaging techniques are involved in every treatment session. High beams of radiation which have lot of energy are used to control cancer cell growth and non-cancer tumors.


Internal radiation or brachytherapy

High-dose-rate (HDR)
