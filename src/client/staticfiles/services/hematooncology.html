Overview

Overview

Hematology-Oncology is a medical specialty of diagnosing, treating, and preventing


Blood diseases (hematology)


Blood cancers (oncology)


These include blood cancers and disorders like

  • Leukemia, Lymphoma, Multiple myeloma
  • Disorders associated with low hemoglobin (types of anemia)
  • Bleeding disorders
  • Other rare diseases

At Kaizen Hematology-Oncology Network, we provide advanced care for all hemato-oncological conditions by top-class physicians who have the expertise and considerable experience. We aim to deliver the best oncology care in a supportive and compassionate way, to all our patients. We are broadening our efforts into hematopoietic stem cell transplants such as bone marrow, peripheral blood stem cell and umbilical cord blood transplants, through innovative research.


We are specialized in treating various conditions like:


Acute and chronic leukemia

Leukemia is a condition that develops in the blood-forming cells, bone marrow and the lymphatic system. Acute leukemia is common among children and increases at a very fast rate. Chronic leukemia develops relatively slower and is commonly seen in adults. Our multidisciplinary team coordinate and work and plan a patient’s overall treatment plan that combines different kinds of treatments. This plan usually includes management of symptoms and side effects, different treatment options, and overall health care.


Acute Leukemia Treatment

The primary treatment for acute lymphocytic leukemia (ALL) is a long-term Chemotherapy care. The typical treatment process happens in 3 phases.

  • Remission induction
  • Intensification
  • Maintenance

    • Chronic Leukemia Treatment

    There are 6 treatment options

    • Watchful waiting
    • Targeted therapy
    • Radiation therapy
    • Chemotherapy
    • Chemotherapy (Bone marrow/stem cell transplant)
    • Chemo-Immunotherapy

    Myelomas and Plasma Cell Disorders

    Myelomas is a blood cancer that begins in the bone marrow cells known as plasma cells. Plasma cell disorders are also known as plasma cell proliferative diseases. It is a group of conditions caused due to monoclonal proliferation of plasma cells resulting in the production of large quantity of antibodies.

Myeloproliferative Neoplasms

This is a blood cancer that is rare and occurs when the body makes too many white blood cells, red blood cells, or platelets. Chronic myeloproliferative neoplasms occur in 6 different types.

  • Chronic myelogenous leukemia
  • Chronic neutrophilic leukemia
  • Chronic eosinophilic leukemia
  • Chronic idiopathic myelofibrosis
  • Essential thrombocythemia
  • Polycythemia vera
